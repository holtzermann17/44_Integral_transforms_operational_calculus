Laplace transform of power function


In the defining integral (http://planetmath.org/ImproperIntegral)

ℒ⁢{tr}=∫0∞e-s⁢t⁢tr⁢𝑑t

of the Laplace transform of the power function  t↦tr,  we make the substitution (http://planetmath.org/SubstitutionForIntegration)  u:=s⁢t:

ℒ⁢{tr}=∫0∞e-u⁢(us)r⁢d⁢us=1sn+1⁢∫0∞e-u⁢ur+1-1⁢𝑑u

Here we have assumed that  r>-1  and s>0.  According to the definition of the gamma function, the last integral is equal to Γ⁢(r+1).  Thus we obtain

ℒ⁢{tr}=Γ⁢(r+1)sr+1. (1)

The special case  r=-12  gives the result

ℒ⁢{1t}=πs. (2)
