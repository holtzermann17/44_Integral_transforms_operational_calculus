Laplace transform of cosine and sine


We start from the easily formula

eα⁢t↶1s-α  (s>α), (1)

where the curved from the Laplace-transformed function to the original function.  Replacing α by -α we can write the second formula

e-α⁢t↶1s+α  (s>-α). (2)

Adding (1) and (2) and dividing by 2 we obtain (remembering the linearity of the Laplace transform)

eα⁢t+e-α⁢t2↶12⁢(1s-α+1s+α),

i.e.

ℒ⁢{cosh⁡α⁢t}=ss2-α2. (3)

Similarly, subtracting (1) and (2) and dividing by 2 give

ℒ⁢{sinh⁡α⁢t}=as2-α2. (4)

The formulae (3) and (4) are valid for  s>|α|.

There are the hyperbolic identities

cosh⁡i⁢t=cos⁡t,1i⁢sinh⁡i⁢t=sin⁡t

which enable the transition from hyperbolic to trigonometric functions.  If we choose  α:=i⁢a  in (3), we may calculate

cos⁡a⁢t=cosh⁡i⁢a⁢t↶ss2-(i⁢a)2=ss2+a2,

the formula (4) analogously gives

sin⁡a⁢t=1i⁢sinh⁡i⁢a⁢t↶1i⋅i⁢as2-(i⁢a)2=as2+a2.

Accordingly, we have derived the Laplace transforms

ℒ⁢{cos⁡a⁢t}=ss2+a2, (5)
ℒ⁢{sin⁡a⁢t}=as2+a2, (6)

which are true for  s>0.
